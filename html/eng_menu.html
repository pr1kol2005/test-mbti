Diagnostics of types according to the Myers-Briggs system is designed to determine one of 16 personality types. It includes 8 scales combined in pairs. The purpose of typology and tests is to help a person determine his individual preferences by establishing which poles of the scales correspond to him more.
1. Scale E—I - orientation of consciousness:
Е (Еxtraversion) — orientation of consciousness outward, towards objects,
I (Introversion) — orientation of consciousness inward, towards the subject;
2. Scale S—N — way of orientation in the situation:
S (Sensing) — orientation to material information,
N (Intuition) — orientation to intuitive information;
3. Scale T—F — decision-making basis:
T (Thinking) — logical weighing of alternatives;
F (Feeling) — decision-making on an emotional basis of ethics;
4. Scale J—P — way of preparing solutions:
J (Judging) — rational preference for planning and organizing information in advance,
P (Perception) — irrational preference to act without detailed preliminary preparation, more focusing on the circumstances.
The combination of scales gives the designation of one of 16 types, for example: ENTP, ISFJ, etc.
